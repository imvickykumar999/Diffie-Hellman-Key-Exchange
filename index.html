Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

Interactive visualization of secure key agreement over an insecure channel.

Public Modulus (p)
23
Base/Generator (g)
5

These numbers are known by everyone, including Eve (the eavesdropper).

Alice

Private & Secure

Calculates Public Key (A)

A = ga mod p
A = 56 mod 23 = 8

Received from Bob (B)

19

Calculated Shared Secret (s)

s = Ba mod p
s = 196 mod 23 = 2

Eve (Eavesdropper)

Intercepted Data

A: 8
B: 19
Eve knows A and B, but cannot find the secret without solving the Discrete Log Problem!

Bob

Private & Secure

Calculates Public Key (B)

B = gb mod p
B = 515 mod 23 = 19

Received from Alice (A)

8

Calculated Shared Secret (s)

s = Ab mod p
s = 815 mod 23 = 2

How it works mathematically:

Both Alice and Bob end up with the same shared secret s because of the laws of exponents in modular arithmetic:

(ga)b mod p = gab mod p = (gb)a mod p

In this example, $p=23$ and $g=5$. If Alice chooses $a=6$ and Bob chooses $b=15$, they compute $A=8$ and $B=19$. When they exchange these, Alice calculates $19^6 \pmod{23}$ and Bob calculates $8^{15} \pmod{23}$. Both equal $2$.
